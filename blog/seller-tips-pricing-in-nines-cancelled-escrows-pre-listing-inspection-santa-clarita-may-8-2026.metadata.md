### LinkedIn

Santa Clarita seller's market report — week of May 8, 2026.

Roughly 90 price reductions in the past 7 days. 65 closed sales. 45 new escrows opened. The market is moving, but the data is asking sellers a question they need to answer with intent rather than instinct.

A few of the takeaways from this week's analysis:

1. Price reductions are corrections, not defeats. About 1 in 5 active listings adjusted price this week. Most of those traced back to listings that priced at the comps on day one but did not adjust as comparable sales shifted underneath over the following month.

2. The reduction that crosses a psychological round number outperforms the one that does not, even when the dollar amount is smaller. $760,000 to $749,900 ($10,100 reduction) will outperform $760,000 to $755,000 ($5,000 reduction) by surfacing in $750K-and-under search filters.

3. Roughly 1 in 3 escrows nationally cancel before close, and the predominant cause is buyer-side credit changes during escrow — new vehicle loans, store credit cards, paid-off accounts that re-score the FICO. Sellers cannot prevent these. They can plan for them by keeping the original offer pool warm and using the Notice to Perform mechanism when warranted.

4. The single most underused tool in a seller's toolbox is the $400-$700 pre-listing inspection. Sellers who address findings with licensed contractors and document the work eliminate the request-for-repairs fight that kills so many deals.

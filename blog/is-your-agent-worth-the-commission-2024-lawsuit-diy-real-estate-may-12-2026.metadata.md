### LinkedIn

The 2024 commission lawsuit changed real estate. Here's what it actually did — and what it didn't.

**What it did:**
1. Made commission negotiability explicit and disclosed in writing on both sides of every transaction.
2. Required written buyer representation agreements before showings in most jurisdictions.
3. Decoupled buyer-broker compensation from the listing-side commission split — buyer compensation is now a separate, transparent agreement between the buyer and their representation.
4. Gave sellers strategic control over what (if anything) they offer to a buyer's agent as a negotiated decision rather than a default assumption.

**What it didn't do:**
It didn't invent the idea that commissions are negotiable. They have always been negotiable. The settlement forced clarity into a conversation that should have been clear from the beginning.

**What it means for consumers:**
If your agent today cannot walk you through their fee structure in plain English, in writing, in the first meeting — that's information. The professional bar has moved up. Agents who were already operating that way didn't change a thing after the settlement. The ones who weren't are now required to.

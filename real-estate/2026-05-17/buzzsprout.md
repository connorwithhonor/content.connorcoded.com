## Episode description (long)
We're past the halfway mark of May 2026, and the headline is split: San Francisco inventory is up — roughly 200 more active units than just a couple of months ago — but the speed at which listings are being absorbed hasn't really shifted since the end of 2025. Real estate is moving, just not at the pace a lot of us had hoped for this spring.

In this episode, Connor walks through:

— The 10-year bond connection. Wars, oil, and fuel pressure are denting confidence in the 10-year, which is dragging mortgage rates the wrong direction. When global jitters settle, expect bond buying to resume and rates to ease.

— A new Fed chair. Confirmed and inbound. Worth watching for the policy adjustments that follow.

— Buyer reality check. Writing offers with zero contingency period puts your deposit on the line the moment escrow opens — before you've read disclosures or hired an inspector. Yes, even buyers in peak-hot Santa Clarita did it; some smartened it up by bringing an inspector to the showing. There's more than one way to move fast without going blind.

— Seller focus: valuations. Get the number right. And know this — the second you type your address into Zillow, Redfin, or Realtor.com, you're a tracked target. Your property gets monitored by AI systems across the syndication network, and your social feeds will light up with real estate ads aimed straight at you.

— The local agent argument. A local agent with a different strategy can be worth more right now than any national portal.

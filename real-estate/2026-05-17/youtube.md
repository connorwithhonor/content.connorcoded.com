## Description
San Francisco inventory has climbed by roughly 200 active units over the last couple of months, but the pace of absorption hasn't really changed since the end of 2025. As of today, May 17, 2026, listings are moving — just not at the speed many of us hoped for going into spring.

Why? A lot of it traces back to the 10-year bond. With wars, oil, and fuel pressure rattling the economic outlook, investors aren't piling into the 10-year, and mortgage rates feel that directly. There's also a new Fed chair confirmed, which could shift the picture once they settle in.

In this update I also cover:
• A buyer reality check on writing offers without an investigation contingency
• Why even in the hottest Santa Clarita markets, some buyers bring an inspector to the showing
